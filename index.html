Best known for his portrayal of individuals from real life

Chadwick Boseman was an American actor. He is known for his portrayal of T'Challa /

Black Panther in the Marvel Cinematic Universe from 2016 to 2019, particularly in Black

Panther (2018), and for his starring roles as several pioneering Americans, Jackie

Robinson in 42 (2013), James Brown in Get on Up (2014), and Thurgood Marshall in

Marshall (2017). He also had choice parts in The Express (2008), Draft Day (2014), and

Message from the King (2016). Born in Anderson, South Carolina, he attended Howard

University and studied at the Oxford Mid-Summer Program for acting, before moving to

Los Angeles in 2008 to pursue his craft on the big screen. He died in 2020, after a four

year bout with colon cancer, during which time he had starred in several of the biggest

movies ever made.
